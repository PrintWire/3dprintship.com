About 3D Print Ship

Covering additive manufacturing for the maritime world — from printed propellers and pumps to the on-demand spare parts that keep vessels moving at sea.

OUR STORY

Additive Manufacturing at the Waterline

3D Print Ship covers the full spectrum of additive manufacturing for the maritime sector — naval and commercial — from WAAM propellers and impellers to valves, heat exchangers, fittings, and the on-demand spare parts produced dockside and at sea.

Our editorial team includes marine engineers, naval architects, and materials scientists with real project experience across shipbuilding, offshore, and fleet operations — fluent in class-society requirements, corrosion-resistant alloys, and design-to-fabrication workflows.

PURPOSE

Our Mission

To cover maritime AM from the printed propeller blade to the full engine-room parts inventory with equal depth — the component, the certification, and the material behind them.

Our Vision

To be the authoritative, vendor-independent source for additive manufacturing at sea — connecting shipyards, operators, and class societies to the suppliers reshaping how marine hardware gets made.
